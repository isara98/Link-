01

Study Overview

This study conducted a systematic, cell-by-cell validation of the organization's internal Grading Map against two authoritative reference documents provided by Willis Towers Watson (WTW): the Career Map Structure and the Global Grading for All Jobs PDF. The objective was to determine which positions are correctly graded, which are misaligned, and which fall outside the defined WTW framework entirely.

The validation covered all four area sheets — Admin, Allied Health, Physician, and Nursing — examining a total of 380 position cells. Cells pre-marked in orange by the organization were treated as excluded from validation (denoting positions under review or pending classification). Only black-text positions were subject to assessment.

02

Methodology

📄
Reference Documents
Validation rules were derived exclusively from the WTW Career Map Structure PDF and the Global Grading for All Jobs PDF. No external HR knowledge was applied.
🔍
Two-Stage Check
Each position was validated on two dimensions: (1) structural alignment of the row's Career Level against its assigned Global Grade, and (2) the position title's expected grade range per the WTW Grade table.
🟠
Orange Exclusion Protocol
Orange-colored cells were identified via raw Excel XML style analysis, capturing all three encoding types: explicit RGB, theme-referenced, and style-inherited. All 272 orange position cells were completely excluded from assessment.
🎨
Color-Coded Output
Results were applied as font color changes only — no cell backgrounds or structure were altered. Green = valid, Red = misaligned, Yellow = not defined in framework.

04

Key Observations

Physician sheet is the best-performing area — with 80 valid positions, zero invalid, and only one not-defined cell, the Physician sheet demonstrates near-complete alignment with the WTW framework. This reflects strong application of the P1–P6 career ladder (Registrar through Senior Consultant) and correct use of the M3/M4 managerial band for Chairman and Deputy Chairman roles.
Admin sheet carries the highest volume of issues — 13 invalid and 35 not-defined cells, primarily concentrated in the Support band (U1–U2, Grades 4–6) and the Admin C-band (Grades 8–11). The large number of not-defined positions reflects org-specific titles (e.g. "Quality Senior Specialist", "HCM Specialist", "Billing Controller") that do not appear by name in the WTW Grade table.
Grade boundary violations are the dominant error type in red cells — the most common pattern is a position placed in a row whose grade does not match the expected range for that job title. For example, Office Manager roles placed at Grade 10 (M1 row) when the WTW table defines "Manager" at Grades 12–13 (M2), and Supervisor-level titles placed at Grade 11 (M1) when WTW expects Grades 10–11.
Allied Health sheet shows pharmacist and technologist classification issues — several positions in the T3 band row (Grade 8) include Radiographer, Dental Hygienist, and Technologist variants which the WTW framework places at T2 (Grade 7) or T1 (Grade 6). This suggests a systematic upward shift in some Allied Health classifications.
Nursing sheet is nearly fully aligned — only one red cell was identified (Supervisor, Diabetic Educator and Clinical Lead, Endoscopy placed at Grade 12, whereas Supervisor roles are defined at M1, Grades 10–11). All other Nursing positions correctly follow the N1–N5 career ladder.
Orange exclusions are heavily concentrated in Admin — 74 of the 115 excluded orange cells reside in the Admin sheet, suggesting a significant portion of administrative positions are still under review or pending formal classification by the organization.

06

Overall Conclusion

The grading map demonstrates strong overall alignment with the WTW framework, with 77.4% of validated positions correctly graded. The Physician and Nursing sheets in particular show a high degree of framework adherence, reflecting disciplined application of the clinical career ladder.

The primary areas requiring attention are the Admin sheet's Support and C-band rows, where a combination of grade-boundary violations and org-specific titles that fall outside the WTW framework create both confirmed misalignments and classification gaps. The Allied Health sheet also shows a pattern of pharmacist and technologist roles placed one band higher than the WTW framework defines.

The 41 not-defined positions (yellow) do not necessarily represent errors — many are legitimate org-specific roles that simply have not been formally evaluated against the WTW methodology. These require a structured job evaluation exercise to assign them to the correct band and grade. The 19 invalid positions (red) represent confirmed grade misalignments that should be reviewed and corrected with the relevant department heads.

It is recommended that the organization prioritize a review of the Admin sheet's M1/M2 boundary (Grades 10–13), conduct formal WTW evaluations for the 41 not-defined positions, and revisit the Allied Health T2/T3 classification for technician-level roles before the next salary review cycle.
